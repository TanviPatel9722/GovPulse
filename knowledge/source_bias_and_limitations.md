# Source Bias And Limitations

## Official Statistical Sources

Official sources are preferred for exact metrics, but they can lag current conditions, be revised, and miss policy-specific mechanisms. Use them for baselines, not automatic causality.

## Official Policy Sources

Policy-text sources establish obligations, deadlines, definitions, and enforcement authority. They do not prove implementation outcomes or public reaction.

## Think Tanks

Think tanks can provide useful interpretation, equity analysis, sector context, and public-facing argument structure. Their institutional perspective and methodology should be disclosed. Their numbers should be corroborated before being marked source-backed.

## Consulting Methodology

Consulting examples and economic-impact methodology references can improve structure and uncertainty language. They should not be copied or treated as factual evidence about the policy under review.

## News And Public Comments

News and public comments help detect salience, framing, stakeholder positions, and emerging opposition or support. They are partial signals and can overrepresent organized or highly motivated voices.

## Social Signals

Social media can reveal confusion, misinformation, and rapid amplification. It is not representative public opinion and should never support exact sentiment metrics by itself.

## Background-Only Sources

Investopedia, Reddit, general blogs, and explainers are development aids for terminology and query expansion. They should not appear as primary evidence in final policy analysis.
